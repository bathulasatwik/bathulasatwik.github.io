Research

My current work studies neural representations through mathematical and geometric lenses. I am interested in how architectural structure constrains what learning systems can represent, and in how principled analysis can reveal both expressive power and fundamental limits.

Broadly, I care about theoretical questions in machine learning, especially those connected to geometry, expressivity, and representation structure.

Current Direction

Representation Geometry and Expressivity
I am particularly interested in questions about curvature, structural limits, and how architectural mechanisms shape the intrinsic geometry of learned representations.
Mathematical Foundations of Learning Systems
A recurring theme in my work is to characterize what a learning system can achieve in principle, rather than only how to optimize it empirically.
